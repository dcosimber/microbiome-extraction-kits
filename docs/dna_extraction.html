3.1 Extraction kits

Four commercially available DNA extraction kits were selected to evaluate their performance across different biological matrices, including marine host-associated samples:

  • MasterPureTM Complete DNA and RNA Purification Kit (# MC85200, Epicentre) and Pathogen Lysis Tubes S (50) (# 19091 Qiagen)

  • QIAamp® PowerFecal® Pro DNA Kit (# 51804, Qiagen) = PowerFecal kit

    The first step of the protocol is mechano-chemical cell disruption through bead beating in lysis buffer. The Inhibitor Removal Technology is then used to remove common substances that interfere with downstream PCR applications. DNA from supernatant is captured on MB Spin Column, washed two times and eluted from the silica membrane.

  • DNeasy® PowerSoil® Pro Kit (# 47014, Qiagen) = PowerSoil kit

    The protocol starts with mechano-chemical cell disruption through beat beating in lysis solution. In this kit, Inhibitor Removal Technology is also applied, which should help in removal of organic and inorganic materials such as humic acids, cell debris, and proteins that could interfere with downstream PCR applications. After that, the sample is loaded onto MB Spin Column with silica membrane. DNA is then washed two times and eluted from the silica membrane.

  • PureLink Genomic DNA Mini Kit (# K182002, Invitrogen) = PureLink kit

    Multiple protocols are proposed for this kit. In this case we applied the Gram Positive Bacterial Cell Lysate Protocol for all types of samples. In this protocol, the microorganisms are lysed by a combination of heat and enzymatic disruption (lysozyme and proteinase K). After that, the sample is treated with a cleanup buffer to eliminate PCR inhibitors. The sample is then applied to a PureLink™ spin column. DNA is washed one time and then eluted from the silica membrane.

3.2 DNA quantification

DNA concentration and quality were assessed using two complementary approaches:

  • Nanodrop spectrophotometry, providing estimates of DNA concentration as well as purity ratios (A260/A280 and A260/A230)
  • Qubit dsDNA High Sensitivity Assay (ThermoFisher), providing fluorometric quantification of double-stranded DNA

While Nanodrop measurements were available for all samples, Qubit measurements were only available for a subset of samples. In some cases, Qubit values were reported as out of range, indicating DNA concentrations below the detection threshold.

4.4 Qubit measurements

Qubit measurements were not available for all samples, and a significant proportion of samples fell below the detection threshold. This limits the use of Qubit data as a primary metric for comparison.

Among measurable samples, Qubit-based concentrations broadly followed trends observed with Nanodrop, although differences in sensitivity must be considered

4.6 Overall, the results demonstrate that:

DNA extraction performance is strongly dependent on both the extraction method and the biological matrix. Mechanical lysis-based kits tend to increase variability but may improve DNA recovery in complex samples. Enzymatic extraction provides more consistent results but may be less efficient in challenging matrices. Inhibitor removal efficiency is a critical factor, particularly for mucus-rich samples such as skin.

Taken together, these findings highlight the importance of selecting an extraction method tailored to the sample type in order to ensure reliable and reproducible microbiome profiling.
